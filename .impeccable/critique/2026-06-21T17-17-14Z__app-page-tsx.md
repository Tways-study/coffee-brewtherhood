## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated?** Not on first pass — real, unsanitized review content (including a flat "7.75/10" review and Google-Maps jargon left intact) and a committed navy+pink palette with photography doing real work both read as hand-built. But two concrete tells would catch a stricter critic's eye on a second pass.

**LLM assessment (Assessment A):** Confirmed clean against most of the absolute-ban list — no side-stripe borders, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no hero-metric template, no numbered 01/02/03 scaffolding, no monospace-as-tech-costume, no single-family-by-reflex (Bricolage Grotesque + Geist is a real pairing), no all-caps body copy, no editorial-magazine defaulting, and zero-imagery is a non-issue (6 real photos in active use). Two real violations against the project's _own_ stated rules: the "CAFE · JARO, ILOILO CITY" line above the H1 is the canonical uppercase-tracked eyebrow DESIGN.md explicitly bans, and the three Reviews cards share identical structure (`rounded-2xl border border-paper/12 bg-surface p-7`) — the "identical card grid" pattern banned elsewhere but not applied to this section.

**Deterministic scan (Assessment B, static):** `detect.mjs` against `app/` returned exit code 0, zero findings. The static scanner doesn't catch semantic patterns like "this card grid is identical" or "this is an eyebrow" from markup alone — it's a clean miss, not a contradiction.

**Browser-runtime evidence (Assessment B, live page):** Injecting the live detector overlay into the running page (`http://localhost:3000`) surfaced **1 runtime finding**: `overused-font — Primary font: geist (78% of text)`. I judge this a **likely false positive**: DESIGN.md deliberately pairs Bricolage Grotesque (display/headings) with Geist Sans (body/UI), and body text legitimately dominates character count on any display+body pairing. This isn't single-family-by-reflex; it's the intended pairing working as designed.

The same browser session also surfaced a **genuine, separate issue** the impeccable detector doesn't check for: Next.js's own console flagged the hero logo (`logo.jpg`, rendered via `next/image`) as the page's Largest Contentful Paint element with no `priority`/eager-loading prop set — a real Core Web Vitals regression on the page's own most-important visual.

I visually re-verified two of Assessment A's open questions directly against screenshots at 1440px and 390px:

- **Logo circle-crop concern (raised by A): resolved, not an issue.** The source logo is already a circular mark on navy — cropping it into `rounded-full` doesn't clip any letters at either the 80px (hero) or 36px (footer) size.
- **Mobile CTA-row concern (raised by A for the Casey persona): partially resolved.** At 390px the three Hero CTAs stack cleanly in a vertical column rather than wrapping unpredictably — better than A's code-only read suggested, though the three different affordance styles (filled pill / outline pill / underlined text) in one stack still cost a beat of parsing.

## Overall Impression

The page clears the generic-AI-slop bar on first impression — real content, a committed palette, genuine photography — but it fails its own brief in two self-inflicted ways (the eyebrow, the review grid) and, more importantly, drops the ball on the two questions a real visitor actually opens the page to answer: **is it open right now**, and **can I actually get delivery**. Those are P0s because they undermine the page's stated job (PRODUCT.md: "a visitor leaves knowing exactly where to go, what to order, and what the place feels like") — right now a visitor cannot reliably determine either.

## What's Working

1. **Unsanitized, real review content.** Keeping Adam Worldz's flat "7.75/10" and the Google-Maps "Local Guide" meta line instead of polishing them into marketing-speak is a genuinely bold, anti-mega-chain choice that earns the "independent cafe, not franchise" positioning PRODUCT.md asks for.
2. **Color/type discipline matches DESIGN.md exactly.** Verified the OKLCH tokens in `globals.css` against spec — the pink accent really is reserved for CTA fills and star ratings only, never decorative filler.
3. **The asymmetric drink gallery** (`DrinkGallery.tsx`) is the one section that actually executes the "no identical grids" principle the brief demands — mixed tile sizes via `row-span`/`col-span`, not a uniform card wall.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] "No-contact delivery" is advertised three times with no way to act on it.** It appears in the Hero subhead, the QuickFacts pills, and the Visit footer — but no link, delivery app, or contact channel is ever given. A visitor who came specifically for delivery hits a dead end exactly at the moment of intent.
_Fix:_ link it to a real channel (Messenger, a delivery app) or demote the claim to a single footnote ("message us on Facebook to arrange delivery") instead of repeating an unactionable promise three times.
_Suggested command:_ `/impeccable clarify`

**[P0] Hours information can't answer "is it open right now."** `content.ts`'s `hoursNote: "Opens 8 AM Mon"` is a single Monday-opening fragment with no closing time and no other days — visibly copy-pasted from the Maps listing rather than translated into usable copy. This fails the page's primary stated job at its highest-stakes decision point.
_Fix:_ source the cafe's actual full operating hours (even coarse-grained, e.g. "Daily, 8 AM – 9 PM") rather than the single fragment currently shown twice (QuickFacts, Visit).
_Suggested command:_ `/impeccable clarify`

**[P1] Reviews section is an identical 3-card grid — the project's own banned pattern, just with real content.** `Reviews.tsx`: all three cards share `rounded-2xl border border-paper/12 bg-surface p-7` and identical internal structure. DESIGN.md bans this exact pattern elsewhere (the drink gallery deliberately avoids it) but it wasn't applied here.
_Fix:_ break the symmetry — pull one review out as a larger pull-quote treatment (matching the Story section's voice) and size the remaining two smaller/secondary.
_Suggested command:_ `/impeccable layout`

**[P1] The Hero category line is a banned eyebrow label.** `Hero.tsx`: `font-display text-sm font-medium uppercase tracking-wide text-muted` for "Cafe · Jaro, Iloilo City" is the canonical AI-SaaS eyebrow pattern, just relocated above the H1 instead of inside a card. Visually confirmed in the 1440px screenshot.
_Fix:_ fold "Cafe · Jaro, Iloilo City" into the rating badge or the subhead line; drop the standalone uppercase-tracked treatment.
_Suggested command:_ `/impeccable typeset`

**[P1] Hero logo is the page's LCP element with no priority loading.** Confirmed via live browser console: Next.js itself flags `logo.jpg` as Largest Contentful Paint with no `loading="eager"`/`priority` set, while the hero background photo already correctly uses `priority`. This is a real, measurable performance regression on the page's own most class-leading visual.
_Fix:_ add the `priority` prop to the logo's `next/image` instance in `Hero.tsx`.
_Suggested command:_ `/impeccable optimize`

**[P2] The most-emphasized menu item has no photo while less-emphasized ones do.** `content.ts` calls Spanish Latte "an all-time visitor favorite" and gives it the largest gallery slot, but it's one of three text-only tiles — while Blue Mansi and Matcha Latte, mentioned less, carry real photography. Hierarchy says one thing; visuals say another.
_Fix:_ source or commission a Spanish Latte photo before shipping; it's the explicit headline drink.
_Suggested command:_ `/impeccable polish`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (first-timer):** Reads "Opens 8 AM Mon" and cannot determine if the cafe is open right now on any other day, or what time it closes. Combined with the unactionable delivery claim, both of Jordan's first two questions ("when can I go," "can I get this delivered") stall immediately. Jordan also hits "Local Guide · 76 reviews · 416 photos" in the Adam Worldz review with zero explanation of what a Local Guide badge means.

**Riley (stress tester):** All three social links in `Footer.tsx` are built from one assumed-identical `profileSlug` string across Facebook/TikTok/Instagram with no verification in the code that the handle is actually correct on every platform — if any one differs, it silently 404s. Riley also flags that every `target="_blank"` link (Hero, Visit, Footer) gives no visual or screen-reader cue that it opens a new tab.

**Casey (distracted mobile user):** Largely fine on mobile — the 390px screenshot shows Hero's three CTAs stacking cleanly rather than wrapping chaotically, better than the source code alone suggested. The remaining friction: three different affordance styles (filled pill / outline pill / underlined text link) stacked back-to-back still cost a beat of visual parsing for someone skimming one-handed, and QuickFacts immediately follows with another pill row — two "parse small pill choices" moments in Casey's first two screens.

## Minor Observations

- `Reviews.tsx` hardcodes "What our reviewers are saying" as a literal string instead of interpolating `business.reviewCount`, while every other instance in the codebase correctly references the constant — a latent data-consistency bug if the count is ever updated.
- The detector's `overused-font` (geist 78%) finding is a likely false positive given the intentional Bricolage Grotesque + Geist display/body split — not flagged as a priority issue, noted for completeness.
- No skip-to-content link for keyboard users on a long single-scroll page with no nav.

## Questions to Consider
