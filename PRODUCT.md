# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People in and around Jaro, Iloilo City browsing Google Maps or social media for a place to get coffee, study, work, or hang out. They're deciding between "is this worth the trip" (location, hours, what to order) and "does this match my vibe" (atmosphere, personality) at the same time — the landing page needs to answer both without making them choose.

## Product Purpose

A standalone marketing site for Coffee Brewtherhood, an independent specialty-coffee cafe. It exists to convert a Google Maps listing's worth of scattered facts (hours, menu, reviews, location) into one coherent, distinctive page that drives both foot traffic and brand/social following. Success: a visitor leaves knowing exactly where to go, what to order, and what the place feels like — and it reads as a real independent cafe's site, not a template.

## Brand Personality

Bold, modern, specialty-coffee-confident. The hand-lettered logo is playful, but the site itself should feel more like a serious third-wave coffee brand than a twee/quirky one — confidence and craft first, charm as an undertone rather than the headline.

## Anti-references

Generic AI SaaS landing page (cream/sand backgrounds, gradient text, eyebrow labels, hero-metric clichés, identical card grids). Also avoid the polished, corporate, mega-chain coffee feel — this is an independent local cafe, not a franchise.

## Design Principles

- Real content only: every fact, review, and image comes from the actual listing/assets — no invented hours, stats, or testimonials.
- Photography carries the warmth; the palette stays committed and confident rather than hedging toward safe neutrals.
- One coherent narrative arc per scroll, not a stack of interchangeable sections.
- Discovery and brand storytelling get equal weight — neither a pure directory page nor a pure mood piece.
- Distinctiveness over safety: bold color and type choices over the generic AI-landing-page average.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: contrast ratios (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text), keyboard navigability, full `prefers-reduced-motion` support. No additional stated requirements.
